Osman Goni Ridwan

AI + Materials Discovery • Generative Models • Crystal Structure Prediction

PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering (UNC Charlotte; GPA 4.00/4.00). I build symmetry-aware generative models for crystals (LEGO-xtal) and high-throughput optimization workflows using ASE, VASP, and ML force fields (MACE / ALIGNN-FF). I collaborate across Computer Science and Computational Chemistry to accelerate functional materials discovery.

Research Experience (selected)

  • LEGO-xtal: symmetry-aware AI framework for crystal generation targeting specific local environments; enabled generation of 1,700+ novel sp²-carbon allotropes from only 25 seeds (first-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • PyXtal-DFTB: automated elastic constants for organic crystals; collaboration with CUNY on mechanical properties of peptide hydrates (co-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • Symmetry-driven structure generator in PyXtal: leveraged crystallographic symmetry; collaboration with LLNL to curate ~200 hypothetical boron nitrides for XAS analysis (co-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • Latent-space crystal optimization: 10× more efficient structure optimization vs direct-space (first-authored manuscript in prep.).
  • Reciprocal-space representations: physics-informed 4D scattering-based descriptors for matching/reconstruction (first-authored manuscript in prep.).
