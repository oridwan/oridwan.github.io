Osman Goni Ridwan

AI + Materials Discovery • Generative Models • Crystal Structure Prediction

PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering (UNC Charlotte). I build symmetry-aware generative models for crystals (LEGO-xtal) and high-throughput optimization workflows using ASE, VASP, and ML force fields (MACE / ALIGNN-FF). I collaborate across Computer Science and Computational Chemistry to accelerate functional materials discovery.

University of North Carolina at Charlotte · Materials Modeling and Informatics (MMI) Lab

Publications Download CV Google Scholar

Research Interests

  • Atomistic materials modeling & crystal structure prediction
  • AI generative models for materials (diffusion, LLMs, VAE with symmetry-aware representations)
  • Energy, molecular and battery materials

Highlights

  • LEGO‑xtal: symmetry‑aware generative framework; 1,700+ novel sp²‑carbon allotropes from 25 seeds; efficient local‑environment targeting.
  • ML Force Fields: staged screening with ALIGNN‑FF → refinement with MACE; robust PES navigation for CSP workflows.
  • HPC Workflows: automated ASE/VASP relaxations, dataset curation (tens of thousands of CIFs), and analysis pipelines.

Education

  • B.Sc., Naval Architecture & Marine Engineering, BUET — 2017–2022; Thesis on earth-pipe cooling system thermal performance (MARTEC 2022 proceedings, 2023).

Research Experience (selected)

  • LEGO-xtal: symmetry-aware AI framework for crystal generation targeting specific local environments; enabled generation of 1,700+ novel sp²-carbon allotropes from only 25 seeds (first-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • PyXtal-DFTB: automated elastic constants for organic crystals; collaboration with CUNY on mechanical properties of peptide hydrates (co-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • Symmetry-driven structure generator in PyXtal: leveraged crystallographic symmetry; collaboration with LLNL to curate ~200 hypothetical boron nitrides for XAS analysis (co-authored manuscript under review). Code
  • Reciprocal-space representations: physics-informed 4D scattering-based descriptors for matching/reconstruction (first-authored manuscript in prep.).
  • Latent space exploration: investigating crystal representations in latent space for enhanced optimization (in preparation).
  • Developing crystal structure determination from PXRD by incorporating physical knowledge into existing LLM foundation models, with plans to integrate agentic systems to enhance model accuracy.
